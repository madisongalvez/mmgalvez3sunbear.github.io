Banh Mi

Banh mi is known for its balance of sweet, savory, and sometimes spicy elements, making it a popular and satisfying street food in Vietnam and an internationally appreciated sandwich. It’s a great example of the diverse influences on Vietnamese cuisine, combining French and indigenous flavors to create a truly unique culinary experience.

Ingredients

For the Baguette

  • 4-6 Vietnamese baguettes or French baguettes, sliced lengthwise
  • Butter or mayonnaise for spreading

For the Filling (Customizable, here's a traditional option)

  • 1 pound pork (can be pork belly, pork shoulder, or pork loin), thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Sliced cucumber
  • Pickled daikon and carrot
  • Sliced jalapeños or chili peppers
  • Fresh cilantro leaves
  • Sliced fresh chili peppers (optional)

For the Pickled Daikon and Carrot

  • 1 cup julienned daikon radish
  • 1 cup julienned carrot
  • 1/2 cup rice vinegar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Preparation

1

Marinate the Pork

  1. Mix soy sauce, fish sauce, minced garlic, and sugar in a bowl. Add the thinly sliced pork and marinate for at least 30 minutes.

2

Make the Pickled Daikon and Carrot

  1. In a saucepan, combine rice vinegar, water, sugar, and salt. Bring to a boil, stirring until the sugar dissolves.
  2. Place the julienned daikon and carrot in a glass jar or bowl. Pour the hot vinegar mixture over them. Allow it to cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least an hour before using.

3

Cook the Pork

  1. Heat oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add the marinated pork and cook until it's fully cooked and slightly caramelized. Set aside.

4

Assemble the Banh Mi

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Split the baguettes lengthwise, spread butter or mayonnaise on each side, and place them on a baking sheet.
  2. Toast the baguettes in the oven for about 5-7 minutes until they're crispy on the outside.

5

Fill the Baguettes

  1. Layer the cooked pork on the bottom half of each baguette.
  2. Add pickled daikon and carrot, sliced cucumber, jalapeños or chili peppers, and fresh cilantro leaves.
  3. Optionally, add sliced fresh chili peppers for extra heat.
