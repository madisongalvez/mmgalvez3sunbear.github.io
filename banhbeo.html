Banh Beo

Banh beo consists of small, round, and translucent steamed rice cakes, typically served in individual bite-sized portions. To eat banh beo, you use a small spoon to scoop out a portion of the rice cake along with the toppings, and then you dip it into the sauce for added flavor. The combination of the soft, slightly chewy texture of the rice cake and the savory toppings makes banh beo a popular and delicious appetizer or snack in Vietnamese cuisine.

Ingredients

For the Batter

  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 2 tablespoons tapioca starch
  • 3 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

For the Toppings

  • Cooked dried shrimp, finely chopped
  • Crispy fried shallots
  • Scallions, thinly sliced
  • Vietnamese pork sausage (cha lua), sliced (optional)
  • Fish sauce dipping sauce (nuoc mam cham)
  • Vegetable oil for greasing the molds

Special Equipment

  • Banh beo molds (small round molds)

Preparation

1

Prepare the Batter

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the rice flour, tapioca starch, sugar, salt, and water. Whisk until the mixture is smooth and well combined. Let the batter rest for about 15-20 minutes.

2

Prepare the Toppings

  1. While the batter is resting, prepare the toppings by chopping the cooked dried shrimp, frying the shallots until crispy, slicing the scallions, and preparing the Vietnamese pork sausage if using.

3

Steam the Banh Beo

  1. Grease the banh beo molds with a little vegetable oil to prevent sticking.
  2. Place the greased molds on a steamer rack or in a steamer basket.
  3. Stir the batter and pour a small amount into each mold, filling it about halfway. Cover the steamer and steam the banh beo for about 5-7 minutes until they are cooked through and set.

4

Serve

  1. Carefully remove the banh beo from the molds onto a serving plate.
  2. Top each banh beo with a small amount of chopped dried shrimp, crispy fried shallots, fried scallions and Vietnamese pork sausage if using.
  3. Serve the banh beo warm with a side of fish sauce dipping sauce (nuoc mam cham) for dipping.
