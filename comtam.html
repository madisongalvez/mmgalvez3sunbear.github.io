Com Tam

Broken Rice

Com Tam or broken rice originally referred to left over rice grains that were broken during handling processes. Poor rural farmers in the Mekong Delta would resort to broken rice as a means to get by during difficult seasons. However, during urbanization to Saigon during the 20th century Com Tam became the widely popular stable dish that it is today. It is commonly paired with grilled pork, an egg meatloaf, shredded pork, lettuce, cucumbers and a dipping sauce, such as fish sauce.

Ingredients

For the Broken Rice (Com Tam)

  • 2 cups of broken rice (com tam), rinsed and drained
  • 4 cups of water
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil

For the Grilled Pork (Com Tam Suon Nuong)

  • 1 pound of pork shoulder or pork chops
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil

For the Egg Meatloaf (Cha Trung)

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup of ground pork
  • 2-3 green onions, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup of wood ear mushrooms, rehydrated and finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil

For the Shredded Pork (Bi Hey)

  • 1 cup of thinly shredded pork
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of fish sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced

For the Nuoc Cham (Dipping Sauce)

  • 2 tablespoons of fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of water
  • 1-2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 Thai bird's eye chilies, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of lime juice

For the Garnish and Accompaniments

  • Fresh cucumber slices
  • Sliced tomatoes
  • Pickled daikon and carrot (do chua
  • Fresh herbs (cilantro, mint, and perilla leaves)
  • Scallion oil (optional)

Preparation

1

Preparing the actual broken rice is fairly simple. As it only requires a rice cooker or pot, along with the broken rice and water.

  1. Wash the excess starches off the rice
  2. Place a 1:1 ratio of water with the rice into your rice cooker or pot
  3. Once the rice has a fluffy consistency, you're done!

2

Preparing the Grilled Pork

  1. In a bowl, mix together the minced garlic, sugar, fish sauce, soy sauce, black pepper, and vegetable oil to make a marinade.
  2. Cut the pork into thin, even slices or chops
  3. Marinate the pork in the sauce for at least 30 minutes, or longer for better flavor
  4. Grill the marinated pork on a barbecue or stovetop grill pan until it's cooked through and slightly caramelized. Set aside

3

Make the Egg Meatloaf (Cha Trung)

  1. In a bowl, beat the eggs
  2. Add ground pork, chopped green onions, wood ear mushrooms (if using), salt, black pepper, and sugar to the beaten eggs. Mix well
  3. Heat a tablespoon of vegetable oil in a non-stick pan over medium heat
  4. Pour the egg mixture into the pan and cook until it is firm and slightly browned on both sides. Flip it carefully to cook evenly
  5. Once cooked, remove the egg meatloaf from the pan, let it cool, then slice it into thin strips

4

Prepare the Shredded Pork (Bi)

  1. In a pan, heat 2 tablesoons of vegetable oil over medium heat.
  2. Add minced garlic and stir until fragrant
  3. Add the shredded pork and stir-fry for a few minutes
  4. Add sugar, fish sauce, and black pepper. Stir-fry until the pork is well coated and caramelized. Remove from heat.

5

Make the Nuoc Cham (Dipping Sauce)

  1. In a small bowl, combine fish sauce, sugar, water, minced garlic, minced chili, and lime juice. Stir until the sugar is dissolved.
  2. Adjust the flavors to your liking by adding more sugar, water, or lime juice

6

Assembling the Com Tam Plate

  1. Place a serving of broken rice on a plate
  2. Arrange the grilled pork, egg meatloaf, and shredded pork on top of the rice
  3. Serve with cucumber slices, sliced tomatoes, pickled daikon and carrot, fresh herbs, and scallion oil (if desired)
