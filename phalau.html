Pha Lau

Pha Lau is a dish primarily consisting of various organ meats, often pork or beef, which are simmered until tender and flavorful. The organs typically used can include pieces of liver, intestines, stomach, and sometimes other offal. These ingredients are cut into bite-sized portions and then cooked slowly in a fragrant, spiced broth.

Ingredients

For the Main Dish

  • 1 pound mixed offal (intestines, stomach, etc.), cleaned and cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 pound pork belly or pork shoulder, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 3-4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 2-3 star anise pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon five-spice powder
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped green onions and cilantro for garnish (optional)

For the Coconut Milk Sauce

  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons tapioca starch or cornstarch (dissolved in 1/4 cup water)

Preparation

1

Prepare the Offal and Pork

  1. Clean the offal thoroughly and cut it into bite-sized pieces. Cut the pork belly or pork shoulder into similar-sized pieces.

2

Prepare the Coconut Milk Sauce

  1. In a bowl, mix the coconut milk with the tapioca starch or cornstarch mixture. Set aside.

3

Cook the Main Dish

  1. Heat oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and saut√© until they turn translucent.
  2. Add minced garlic, ginger, star anise, cinnamon stick, and bay leaves. Cook for a couple of minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the pork pieces and stir-fry until they are lightly browned.
  4. Add the mixed offal pieces and continue to cook for a few minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Season with five-spice powder, sugar, fish sauce, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine all ingredients evenly.

4

Simmer and Cook

  1. Pour in enough water to cover the meat and offal. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours until the offal and pork are tender. Add water if needed to maintain the desired consistency.

5

Thicken the Sauce

  1. Once the meat and offal are tender, pour in the coconut milk mixture. Stir well to combine.
  2. Continue simmering for an additional 10-15 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.

6

Serve:

  1. Remove the star anise pods, cinnamon stick, and bay leaves before serving.
  2. Serve Pha Lau hot, garnished with chopped green onions and cilantro if desired. It's typically served with French bread or steamed rice.
