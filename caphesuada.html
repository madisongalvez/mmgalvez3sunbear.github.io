Vietnamese Coffee

Vietnamese coffee is known for its strong, bold flavor, often described as dark, rich, and slightly nutty. Itâ€™s both sweet and robust, making it a popular choice for those who enjoy a sweet and intense coffee experience. The slow drip brewing method and the use of condensed milk give Vietnamese coffee a unique and memorable taste that has captivated coffee enthusiasts worldwide.

Ingredients

What You Need

  • Coarsely ground Vietnamese coffee beans (often a mix of robusta and arabica)
  • Sweetened condensed milk
  • Hot water (near boiling)
  • Vietnamese coffee filter (phin)

Preparation

1

Set Up the Vietnamese Coffee Filter (Phin)

  1. Take apart the phin filter chamber and place it on top of your coffee cup or glass.
  2. Add about 2 tablespoons of coarsely ground Vietnamese coffee into the filter chamber, spreading it out evenly.

2

Assemble the Phin

  1. Place the perforated press inside the filter chamber and press down gently to compress the coffee grounds.

3

Pour Hot Water

  1. Heat the water to near boiling.
  2. Pour a small amount of hot water (just enough to wet the coffee grounds) into the filter chamber.
  3. Let it sit for about 20-30 seconds to allow the coffee grounds to bloom.

4

Brew the Coffee

  1. After the blooming period, slowly pour the rest of the hot water into the filter chamber. Fill it to the top.
  2. Place the lid on top to retain heat and let the coffee drip through the grounds. This process might take around 5-7 minutes.

5

Mix and Serve

  1. While the coffee is brewing, prepare the sweetened condensed milk in your cup or glass.
  2. Once all the water has dripped through, remove the filter chamber and stir the brewed coffee and condensed milk together.

6

Enjoy

  1. Vietnamese coffee can be served hot or over ice. If serving over ice, simply pour the brewed coffee over a glass filled with ice cubes.
