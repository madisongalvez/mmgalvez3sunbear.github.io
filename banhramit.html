Banh Ram it

Banh ram it is a dish of sticky rice dumplings that are filled with sautéed shrimp and pork, which is also stacked on top of a crispy rice cake. The dish is loved for its combination of textures - the chewy dumplings, the crispy exterior, and the various toppings that offer both depth of flavor and visual appeal. It’s a popular street food in Vietnam and is often enjoyed as a snack or appetizer.

Ingredients

For the Dumpling Dough

  • 1 cup glutinous rice flour
  • 1/4 cup rice flour
  • 1 cup boiling water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

For the Filling

  • 1/2 pound ground pork or shrimp (or a combination)
  • 1/4 cup wood ear mushrooms (soaked in warm water until soft, then finely chopped)
  • 1/4 cup shallots, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Ground black pepper to taste

For the Topping

  • 1 cup mung bean paste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup crispy fried shallots
  • Chopped green onions
  • Fresh herbs (cilantro, mint)

Nam Giao is a casual restuarant with a large variety of dishes originating from Hue. Sunbear's recommendations are their banh ram it and banh beo.

Preparation

1

Prepare the Dumpling Dough

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the glutinous rice flour, rice flour, and salt.
  2. Gradually pour in the boiling water while stirring continuously until a dough forms. Knead the dough until smooth. Cover and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.

2

Make the Filling

  1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add shallots and garlic, sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add the ground pork or shrimp and cook until it's no longer pink. Stir in the chopped wood ear mushrooms, fish sauce, and black pepper. Cook until the mixture is well combined and cooked through. Set aside.

3

Shape the Dumplings

  1. Pinch off small pieces of the dough and roll them into small balls, about 1 inch in diameter. Flatten each ball into a thin round disc, about 2 inches in diameter.

4

Assemble the Dumplings

  1. Place a spoonful of the filling in the center of each dough disc. Fold the edges up to create a dumpling shape, ensuring the filling is enclosed.

5

Cook the Dumplings

  1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat for frying. Fry the dumplings in batches until they turn golden brown and crispy on the outside. Remove and drain excess oil on paper towels.

6

Prepare the Topping

  1. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a separate pan. Add the mung bean paste and cook it for a few minutes until heated through.
