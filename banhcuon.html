Banh Cuon

Vietnamese Rice Rolls

Banh cuon is a traditional Vietnamese dish that consists of delicate, thin rice flour sheets or rolls, often likened to rice crepes. The term "banh cuon" translates to "rolled cake" in English, which aptly describes the preparation method.

Ingredients

For the Rice Crepes

  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 2 cups of water
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • Vegetable oil (for brushing the steamer)

For the Grilled Pork (Com Tam Suon Nuong)

  • 1/2 pound of ground pork
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Ground black pepper to taste
  • Chopped green onions for garnish (optional)
  • Fried shallots for garnish (optional)

For Serving

  • Vietnamese ham (cha lua), sliced
  • Bean sprouts
  • 2-3 green onions, finely chopped
  • Fresh herbs (cilantro, mint, perilla leaves)
  • Nuoc cham dipping sauce (prepared or store-bought)

Preparation

1

Prepare the Rice Crepes

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the rice flour, water, and salt. Whisk until smooth to create a thin batter.
  2. Heat a non-stick skillet or a banh cuon steamer over medium heat. Brush the surface with a little oil.
  3. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the skillet, tilting it to spread the batter thinly and evenly. Cover with a lid and steam for a minute or until the crepe sets. Remove the crepe carefully with a spatula and place it on a plate. Repeat until all the batter is used.

2

Make the Filling

  1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add shallots and garlic, sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add the ground pork and cook until it's no longer pink. Stir in the chopped wood ear mushrooms, fish sauce, and black pepper. Cook for a few minutes until the mixture is well combined and cooked through. Set aside.

3

Assemble the Banh Cuon

  1. Place a spoonful of the pork filling in the center of a rice crepe. Fold the sides of the crepe over the filling to form a roll. Repeat with the remaining crepes and filling.

4

Serve

  1. Arrange the banh cuon rolls on a serving plate.
  2. Serve them with sliced Vietnamese ham, bean sprouts, fresh herbs, and nuoc cham dipping sauce.
  3. Garnish with chopped green onions and fried shallots if desired.
